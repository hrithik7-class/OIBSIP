Biography

Jensen Huang, born on February 17, 1963, in Tainan, Taiwan, is the CEO and president of NVIDIA Corporation, a leading technology company known for its graphics processing units (GPUs). Huang immigrated to the United States as a child, growing up in Oregon. He earned a bachelor’s degree in electrical engineering from Oregon State University in 1984 and a master’s degree from Stanford University in 1992. Huang co-founded NVIDIA in 1993 with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem, aiming to advance graphics technology for computing. Under his leadership, NVIDIA transformed from a niche graphics card manufacturer into a global powerhouse in GPUs, artificial intelligence (AI), and high-performance computing. Huang’s vision to pivot NVIDIA toward AI and deep learning, particularly with the development of CUDA and data center solutions, propelled the company to the forefront of the AI revolution. Innovations like the GeForce GPUs and DGX AI supercomputers solidified NVIDIA’s dominance. Known for his hands-on leadership and iconic leather jacket, Huang has been recognized as one of the world’s most influential CEOs, earning accolades like Fortune’s Businessperson of the Year in 2017. As of 2025, his net worth exceeds $100 billion, reflecting NVIDIA’s meteoric rise. Huang remains a driving force in shaping AI and computing’s future.
